Rafting Info

Idaho is known as the “Whitewater State” for a reason, offering some of the best rafting experiences in the country. The Salmon River, also known as the “River of No Return,” provides breathtaking scenery and thrilling rapids suitable for all skill levels. For those seeking an even wilder ride, the Payette River near Boise offers multiple sections, such as the North Fork and South Fork, each with varying degrees of difficulty. Additionally, the Snake River near Hells Canyon, the deepest river gorge in North America, delivers a mix of tranquil waters and adrenaline-pumping rapids. Whether you’re a beginner or an expert, Idaho’s diverse rivers have something for everyone.

Getting started with white river rafting is easier than you might think. If you’re new to the sport, begin by booking a guided rafting trip with a reputable outfitter. These tours provide all the necessary equipment, experienced guides, and thorough safety instructions. Many companies in Idaho, such as ROW Adventures and Cascade Raft & Kayak, cater to beginners and even families. If you want to build your skills, consider taking a whitewater rafting class, where you can learn paddling techniques, safety procedures, and how to read the river. Over time, you can graduate to tackling more challenging rapids on your own or with a group.

Proper gear is essential for a safe and enjoyable white river rafting experience. At a minimum, you’ll need a high-quality personal flotation device (PFD), a helmet, and a paddle. Wetsuits or drysuits are recommended for cold water conditions, along with river shoes that provide grip and protection. Sunscreen, quick-drying clothing, and a hat with a secure strap are important for sun protection, while a waterproof dry bag is ideal for keeping valuables safe. If you’re venturing without a guide, you’ll also need a durable inflatable raft, a pump, and a first aid kit. Always ensure your gear is in good condition and suited for the river you plan to navigate.
